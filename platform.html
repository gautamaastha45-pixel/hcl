02 The operating system

Service Model Management System™

Every act of work is a service. Every service has a customer. Every customer defines value.

SMMS™ is the management system that replaces the job-based operating model. Instead of organizing work around roles, departments, and hierarchy, SMMS™ organizes work around services, customers, and outcomes.

This is not a consulting framework or a set of principles. It is a practical operating model that organizations can implement, measure, and continuously improve.

Five principles

1

Every act of work is a service

Work is not defined by a job description. It is defined by the service being delivered. Each service has a clear purpose, a defined customer, and a measurable outcome.

2

Every service has a customer

Internal or external, every service exists to create value for someone. When customers are clearly defined, accountability becomes natural and value becomes measurable.

3

Every customer defines value

Value is not determined by the provider. It is determined by the customer. This creates a direct link between organizational activity and business outcomes.

4

Talent and AI are integrated

Within every service, human talent and AI capabilities are combined as a single work unit. This is not about automation. It is about integration.

5

Adaptation is continuous

Services are designed to learn from every interaction. Customer feedback, performance data, and AI insights create compounding improvement. The system gets better as it operates.

04 The collaboration model

Human Equivalence™

A framework for understanding how humans and AI collaborate while maintaining accountability, trust, and organizational performance.

As AI takes on more work, organizations face a new set of questions:

  • Who is accountable when a Talent+AI™ work unit makes a decision?
  • How do you measure the contribution of AI versus human talent?
  • How do you allocate resources when work is done by both people and machines?
  • How do you maintain trust when AI is deeply embedded in service delivery?
  • How do you govern an organization where the workforce is a blend of human and AI?

Human Equivalence™ provides a structured way to answer these questions. It creates a common language for understanding what humans contribute, what AI contributes, and how accountability works within integrated Talent+AI™ work units.

Why this matters

Resource allocation

Move from headcount-based budgeting to value-based resource allocation that accounts for both talent and AI capability.

Accountability

Clear lines of responsibility within Talent+AI™ work units, even when AI is performing significant parts of the work.

Trust

Organizational confidence in AI-integrated services because the collaboration model is transparent and governed.

Performance

Understanding how to improve Talent+AI™ work units by knowing what each component contributes to the outcome.

05 The measurement

ROTA™

Return on Talent and AI. A new way to measure organizational performance in the AI era.

Traditional metrics were designed for traditional organizations. Revenue per employee. Cost per headcount. FTE ratios. Utilization rates.

These metrics assume the job is the unit of work.

When the unit of work becomes Talent+AI™, you need a different way to measure performance. One that captures the value created by integrated human and AI work, not just the cost of either.

What ROTA™ measures

Traditional metrics

  • Revenue per employee
  • Cost per headcount
  • FTE ratios
  • Utilization rates
  • Adoption rates
  • License usage

ROTA™

  • Value created per Talent+AI™ work unit
  • Service outcomes delivered
  • Customer value realized
  • Learning velocity
  • Adaptation rate
  • Compounding improvement

ROTA™ gives executives a way to measure what actually matters: the return their organization creates through the integration of talent and AI. It connects AI investment to business outcomes. No more proxy metrics.
