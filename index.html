How it works

Six problems. One operating model.

01

Your organization runs on jobs. But a job isn't a unit of work.

It's a budget line. A headcount number. A role description. It was never designed to describe work itself.

What if every act of work was a service? With a defined customer. A measurable outcome. A clear value exchange.

That's the foundation. We call it the Service Model.

Value creation, instead of cost accounting, is the correct basis for resource allocation. Cost models can't compare human and AI contribution. Value-based service exchange can.
02

Now you have services. Thousands of them. How do you manage them?

Not with another strategy deck. Not with a restructuring.

You need an operating system that works within your existing jobs and functions. That evolves the organization from where it already is. No disruption. No reorganization.

That system is SMMS™.

03

AI is doing real work now. But you can't compare what it contributes to what a person contributes.

Different inputs. Different costs. No common language.

Human Equivalence™ measures AI's contribution in the same terms you use for people: %FTE and margin impact. For the first time, you can see both on the same scorecard.

04

With a common measure, you can do something that was impossible before.

Combine human talent and AI into a single work unit. Not a person using a tool. A designed unit where judgment and capability are integrated.

We call it Talent+AI™. It replaces headcount as the basis for planning.

05

Every CEO asks the same question: what's the return?

ROCE measures capital. ROLE measures labor. Neither measures both.

ROTA™ does. Service value per service. Service profiles per function. Margin contribution at the P&L. One number that tells you whether the integration is working.

Early adopters: 20% service value increase in the first cycle. 5-month payback. 12.9% margin contribution over 18 months.
